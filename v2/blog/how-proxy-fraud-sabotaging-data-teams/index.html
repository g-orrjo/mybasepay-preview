How Proxy Fraud is Sabotaging Data Teams

KP

Karla Perez

myBasePay

The race to build internal AI and machine learning capabilities has never been more intense. Chief Data Officers are under immense pressure to rapidly scale their teams.

The Hidden Threat to AI Teams

The race to build internal AI and machine learning capabilities has never been more intense. Chief Data Officers are under immense pressure to rapidly scale their teams with specialized talent — data scientists, ML engineers, and AI architects who can transform raw data into competitive advantage.

But there's a growing threat that many organizations aren't prepared for: proxy fraud. This is when someone else takes the interview on behalf of the actual candidate, or when candidates misrepresent their skills and experience to land roles they're not qualified for.

How Proxy Fraud Works

In the remote-first world, proxy fraud has become disturbingly easy. A skilled impostor can take a video interview with camera off, complete technical assessments, and even handle initial onboarding tasks — all while the actual "employee" who shows up for work has a fraction of the demonstrated skills.

Common proxy fraud tactics include:

  • Someone else taking the technical interview via screen share or second device
  • AI-assisted answers during live coding assessments
  • Fabricated portfolios and work samples from other developers
  • Identity substitution where a different person attends interviews vs. starts work

The Impact on Data Teams

For data teams specifically, the consequences of proxy fraud are severe. Unqualified team members can introduce errors into models, compromise data pipelines, and create technical debt that takes months to unwind. In regulated industries, flawed data analysis can lead to compliance violations and significant financial penalties.

“One unqualified data scientist can set a team back six months — not just from the bad work they produce, but from the good work that doesn't get done while the team is cleaning up the mess.”

Protecting Your Organization

Organizations can protect themselves by implementing multi-step verification processes, conducting live technical assessments with cameras on, and partnering with workforce providers who vet candidates rigorously.
