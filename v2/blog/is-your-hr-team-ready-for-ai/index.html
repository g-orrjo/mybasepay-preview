Is Your HR Team Ready for AI?

KP

Karla Perez

myBasePay

The rush to adopt AI can often outpace the actual readiness to implement it. Here's how HR leaders can prepare their teams for the transformation ahead.

Understanding the Shift

In an era where technology continuously reshapes how we work, AI stands out as a particularly transformative tool. Many HR teams are enthusiastic about the potential of AI to streamline operations and enhance efficiency. However, the rush to adopt this technology can often outpace the actual readiness to implement it effectively.

Around 80% of organizations are either already investing in AI or plan to do so within the next year. Despite this interest, a disconnect exists between the enthusiasm for AI and the practical readiness for its deployment.

The 7-Question AI Readiness Checklist

To gauge your team's AI readiness, consider:

  • Do we have clear objectives for what we want to achieve with AI?
  • Is our data structured in a way that can be utilized by AI systems?
  • Do we have the necessary policies for data privacy and security?
  • Is there a training plan for our team to learn AI functionalities?
  • Have we evaluated the potential ethical implications of deploying AI?
  • Do we have the technical infrastructure required to support AI systems?
  • Are we prepared to iteratively adjust our AI strategies based on outcomes?

The Premature Implementation Trap

The fear of falling behind in technological advancements can lead HR teams to hastily implement AI solutions without the necessary groundwork. This can result in unreliable insights and, ultimately, limited benefits from AI. Building a solid foundation first ensures that AI tools provide accurate and valuable insights.

“Close to two-thirds of teams believe they will spend excessive time on administrative tasks without AI — but nearly half are wary of the risks to sensitive data. The key is readiness, not speed.”

Getting It Right

Implementing AI in HR isn't just about staying ahead of the curve — it's about being prepared to navigate that curve safely and effectively. By addressing foundational needs first, HR teams can leverage AI not just as a tool for efficiency, but as a catalyst for broader strategic transformation.
