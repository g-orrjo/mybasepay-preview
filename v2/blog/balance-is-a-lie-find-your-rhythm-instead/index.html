Balance is a Lie, Find Your Rhythm Instead

AA

Angela Alberty

CEO, myBasePay

We've all heard of 'work-life balance' — the holy grail we're supposed to chase, the achievement that will finally make us feel like we're doing everything right.

The Balance Myth

We've all heard of 'work-life balance' — the holy grail we're supposed to chase, the achievement that will finally make us feel like we're doing everything right. But here's what nobody tells you: balance is a myth. It implies a static equilibrium where every area of your life gets equal weight at all times. That's not how life works, and it's certainly not how leadership works.

As a CEO and a mother, I've stopped chasing balance. Instead, I've learned to find my rhythm — the cadence that lets me show up fully for what matters most in each season of life, without guilt about what temporarily takes a back seat.

What Rhythm Looks Like

Rhythm acknowledges that some weeks are all-in on work. A product launch, a critical negotiation, a team crisis — these demand your full attention. Other weeks, family comes first. A sick child, a school milestone, a moment that won't come again. Rhythm gives you permission to lean into whatever needs you most, trusting that the pendulum will swing back.

“Balance says you should be everything to everyone, all the time. Rhythm says you can be exactly what's needed, when it's needed — and that's enough.”

The leaders I admire most aren't the ones who maintain perfect balance. They're the ones who are present. Fully present at work when they're working. Fully present at home when they're home. They don't try to split their attention equally — they give their full attention to whatever they're doing.

Building a Team That Supports Rhythm

Finding your rhythm requires having a team you trust. You can't lean into family time if you're worried about what's happening at the office. You can't go all-in on a deal if no one's covering your other responsibilities. Building that trust — and being willing to cover for others when they need it — is what makes rhythm sustainable.

At myBasePay, we've built a culture that supports this. Our team members know that life happens, and we celebrate the moments — personal and professional — that make us whole. Because when people feel supported, they bring their best selves to work. And that benefits everyone.

Permission to Stop Chasing

If you're exhausted from trying to balance it all, give yourself permission to stop. Find your rhythm instead. Trust the seasons. Show up fully. And know that the people who matter most — at work and at home — would rather have all of you some of the time than a fraction of you all of the time.
