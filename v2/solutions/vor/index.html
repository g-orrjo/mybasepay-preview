FAQ

Common Questions About VOR Services

A Vendor of Record is a centralized intermediary that manages your third-party supplier relationships under a single contractual and compliance framework. The VOR consolidates vendor contracts, invoices, and compliance monitoring — giving you full visibility and control over your contingent workforce supply base without the administrative burden of managing each vendor individually.

While both VOR and MSP services help manage your contingent workforce, a VOR focuses specifically on the contractual, financial, and compliance aspects of your vendor relationships. We become the single contracting entity for all your suppliers, consolidate invoicing, and ensure compliance. An MSP typically includes a broader set of workforce management services including requisition management, candidate sourcing, and program governance. myBasePay can provide both services depending on your needs.

We can typically onboard your existing vendor base within 1-2 weeks, depending on the number of suppliers and the complexity of your current arrangements. Our team handles all contract migration, compliance verification, and platform setup. Your vendors experience minimal disruption during the transition.

The day-to-day working relationship between your teams and your vendors remains unchanged. What changes is the administrative layer — contracts, invoicing, and compliance are managed through myBasePay instead of individually. Vendors benefit from faster, more reliable payments and a streamlined administrative process.

Cost savings come from multiple sources: consolidated procurement and volume-based rate negotiation, elimination of rogue or off-contract spend, streamlined invoice processing and payment workflows, reduced administrative overhead, better visibility into rate benchmarking across your vendor portfolio, and identification and removal of redundant supplier relationships.

Every vendor in your supply base is assessed against a comprehensive compliance framework that includes insurance verification, regulatory licensing, labor law adherence, data protection and privacy standards, financial stability, and industry-specific certifications. We continuously monitor compliance status and flag any issues before they become risks.

Absolutely. Our platform is designed to scale with your business. New vendors can be onboarded at any time through a standardized process — compliance vetting, contract execution, and platform integration. This ensures every new supplier meets the same standards as your existing vendor base from day one.
