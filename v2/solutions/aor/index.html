FAQ

Common Questions About AOR Services

An Agent of Record is a third-party intermediary that manages the contractual and payment relationship between your company and your independent contractors. The AOR handles contractor agreements, payments, tax documentation, and compliance — while you retain full control over the contractor's scope of work and deliverables.

Our AOR service includes a rigorous classification assessment for every contractor engagement. We evaluate the relationship against local classification criteria — including behavioral control, financial control, and relationship factors — before any contract is issued. If an engagement presents classification risk, we'll advise on restructuring the arrangement or recommend our EOR service as an alternative.

An AOR manages the engagement of independent contractors — handling contracts, payments, and compliance for non-employee workers. An EOR is the legal employer of full-time employees in a given country. If your worker should be classified as an employee (based on the nature of the engagement), we'll recommend our EOR service to ensure full compliance.

We can typically generate compliant contractor agreements and complete onboarding within 24 hours. For engagements in jurisdictions with additional registration or documentation requirements, your dedicated account manager will provide a specific timeline.

We support payments in 70+ currencies worldwide. Our platform handles currency conversion, payment processing, and reconciliation — so you receive a single consolidated invoice while your contractors are paid in their preferred local currency, on time.

As your Agent of Record, we assume the compliance risk associated with contractor classification. If a local authority challenges the classification of a contractor engaged through our platform, our legal team manages the response and resolution process.
