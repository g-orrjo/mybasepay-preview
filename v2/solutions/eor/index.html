FAQ

Common Questions About EOR Services

An Employer of Record is a third-party organization that becomes the legal employer of your workers in a given country. The EOR handles all employment responsibilities — including payroll, taxes, benefits, and compliance — while you retain full operational control over the employee's day-to-day work. This allows you to hire globally without establishing your own local entities.

Our EOR service covers the full employment lifecycle: compliant employment contracts, payroll processing in local currencies, statutory and supplemental benefits administration, tax withholdings and filings, onboarding and offboarding support, ongoing compliance monitoring, and a centralized platform for managing your entire global workforce.

In most countries, we can onboard a new employee within 48 hours of receiving the required documentation. Some jurisdictions may require additional time for background checks, work permits, or regulatory approvals. Your dedicated account manager will provide a clear timeline for each hire.

An EOR (Employer of Record) is the legal employer of your full-time workers in foreign countries. An AOR (Agent of Record) manages and pays independent contractors on your behalf, protecting you from misclassification risk. A VOR (Vendor of Record) consolidates and manages your third-party supplier relationships, invoicing, and compliance. myBasePay offers all three services as part of our integrated contingent workforce platform.

We maintain a dedicated team of in-country legal and HR experts who monitor regulatory changes across every jurisdiction we operate in. All employment contracts, payroll calculations, benefits packages, and termination procedures are reviewed and updated continuously to ensure full compliance. We also conduct regular audits and provide compliance reporting through your dashboard.

We currently operate in 61+ countries across North America, South America, Europe, Asia-Pacific, the Middle East, and Africa. Our network is growing continuously. If you need to hire in a country not yet on our list, contact us — we can often establish coverage within a matter of weeks.
